# Indigenous Training Programs Across Canada

*Research compiled: January 30, 2026*

---

## Saskatchewan

### SIIT (Saskatchewan Indian Institute of Technologies)
- **Website:** https://siit.ca
- **Type:** Full institution
- **Offerings:**
  - 30+ Academic and Quick Skills programs
  - Adult Basic Education
  - Trades and Industrial programs
  - **JobSeries** - foundational, in-community pre-employment training
  - **JobConnections** - 9 centres province-wide connecting job seekers to employment
  - JobConnections Mobile - career services for rural communities
- **Notes:** 90% Indigenous student body, 60,000+ alumni, 65% Indigenous staff

### Saskatchewan Apprenticeship (SATCC)
- **Website:** https://saskapprenticeship.ca
- **Type:** Provincial certification body
- **Offerings:**
  - Saskatchewan Youth Apprenticeship (SYA) - high school students
  - Apprenticeship programs for 50+ trades
  - Experienced worker certification
- **Notes:** Good for trades career pathways

## Alberta

### Rupertsland Institute (Métis Training to Employment)
- **Website:** https://www.rupertsland.org
- **Type:** ISET holder for Métis in Alberta
- **Offerings:**
  - **Certificate programs** under 52 weeks
  - **Short-term training** for employment/skill development
  - Safety tickets/certifications
  - **Youth Summer Employment Program** (ages 15-30)
  - **Employment Partnership Program** - work experience with wage support
  - **Entrepreneurship workshops** - https://www.metisentrepreneurs.com
  - Mobile employment units serving rural communities
- **Eligibility:** Métis individuals in Alberta
- **Contact:** 1-888-48-MÉTIS (1-888-486-3847)

## National Programs

### Government of Canada (ISC)

#### First Nations & Inuit Skills Link Program
- **Website:** https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100033627/1533125289674
- **Type:** Government funding program
- **Offerings:**
  - Essential employability skills training
  - Mentored work placements (up to 11 months)
  - Career promotion activities
  - Science & technology exposure
  - Co-op education placements
  - Internships
- **Eligibility:** First Nations & Inuit youth ages 15-30
- **Funding:** Up to 100% wage costs (non-profit) or 50% (private sector)
- **Deadline:** March 6, 2026 for 2026-27 proposals

#### First Nations & Inuit Summer Work Experience Program
- **Website:** https://www.sac-isc.gc.ca/eng/1100100033610/1533125433575
- **Type:** Government funding program
- **Offerings:**
  - Summer employment wage subsidies
  - Career and labour market information
  - Summer job placement assistance
- **Eligibility:** First Nations & Inuit secondary/post-secondary students, ages 15-30
- **Requirements:** Minimum 80 hours of work per participant
- **Deadline:** March 6, 2026

### CCIB (Canadian Council for Indigenous Business)
- **Website:** https://www.ccib.ca/programs/
- **Type:** National business organization
- **Programs:**
  - **Tools for Indigenous Business (TIB)** - templates, worksheets, guides for entrepreneurs
  - **Capital Skills** - online learning for Indigenous entrepreneurs/business owners
  - **Schulich Mini-MBA for Indigenous Leaders** - 12 virtual sessions over 3 months
  - **Connects** - industry mentorship (Leaders to Learners)
  - **Certified Indigenous Business (CIB)** - certification program
  - **Indigenous Business Defence Sector Accelerator** - defence industry opportunities
  - PAIR (Partnership Accreditation in Indigenous Relations)
  - Supply Change - procurement strategy program

### NACCA (National Aboriginal Capital Corporations Association)
- **Website:** https://nacca.ca
- **Type:** Network of Indigenous Financial Institutions
- **Programs:**
  - **Indigenous Women's Entrepreneurship (IWE) Program**
  - **Indigenous Youth Entrepreneurship (IYE) Program**
  - Business loans and financing
  - Non-repayable contributions
  - Financial/management consulting
  - Business start-up and aftercare services
- **Resources:** Connect entrepreneurs to local Indigenous Financial Institutions (IFIs)

### Indigenous Clean Energy (ICE)
- **Website:** https://indigenouscleanenergy.com/our-programs/
- **Type:** Clean energy capacity building
- **Programs:**
  - **20/20 Catalysts** - award-winning clean energy leadership program (100+ graduates)
  - **Bringing It Home** - energy efficiency in Indigenous communities
  - **Generation Power** - youth clean energy careers
  - **ImaGENation** - youth clean energy project support
  - **ICE Mentorship** - connects participants with industry experts
  - **Charge Up** - EV charging in Indigenous communities
- **Notes:** Great for environmental/clean energy careers

### TECHNATION Indigenous Pathways
- **Website:** https://technationcanada.ca/en/future-workforce-development/career-ready-program/indigenous_pathways/
- **Type:** Tech industry networking
- **Offerings:**
  - Gatherings and circles connecting Indigenous students with tech industry leaders
  - Career exploration in tech
  - Networking opportunities
- **Notes:** Helps Indigenous students explore tech careers regardless of academic discipline

### Indspire
- **Website:** https://indspire.ca/programs/
- **Type:** Indigenous education charity
- **Programs:**
  - Bursaries, scholarships, and awards
  - Mentoring programs
  - Youth conferences
  - Educator support and resources
- **Notes:** Focus on graduation and career fulfillment

### Mining Industry Human Resources (MiHR)
- **Website:** https://mihr.ca/cmsds/training/training/
- **Type:** Mining industry training
- **Programs:**
  - **Mining Essentials** - work readiness training for Indigenous Peoples
  - **Mining Potential** - essential skills training
  - Occupational training for mining
  - eLearning and microlearning modules
  - Indigenous Awareness eLearning
- **Notes:** Good for mining/resource sector careers

## Additional Resources to Research

### ISET Holders (Indigenous Skills and Employment Training)
Each province/territory has ISET agreement holders who deliver employment training:
- Research local ISET holders for specific regions
- They provide funding for skills training, wage subsidies, job placement

### Tribal Council Training Programs
- Many tribal councils run their own employment and training programs
- Region-specific - need to research by area

### Band-Specific Training
- Individual First Nations often have training budgets
- Usually accessed through band offices

### Safety Certifications (Common Quick Tickets)
- H2S Alive
- First Aid / CPR
- WHMIS
- Fall Protection
- Confined Space Entry
- Forklift / Equipment operation
- Food Safe
- Security Guard certification
